One of the many sacrifices made during the four year "planning for the big cruise" phase was to do minimal technology upgrades - electronics, computers, phones, etc. Aside from things needed for the boat (navigation, radar, etc) we tried to limit purchases to what we really needed bare minimum and just through our planned six-month trip.

As my facebook friends already know from my making good use of the novelty of "checking in", I've got a new phone - an Android powered Samsung Galaxy S. This is a really incredible device... A huge step up from the cheapest Nokia and Samsung phones that we've been using for the past dozen years. The number and richness of available apps is just huge. The GPS navigation means I won't be pulling out my trusty old Garmin GPSMap76CSx very often. I remember how cool I thought this GPS was 4 years ago when I bought it, but there's just no comparison with what these smartphone devices can do...

Access to my personal email, IM, skype and corporate email, calendar, yammer means I can't hide anymore! Both WIFI and USB tethering work out of the box - which means a laptop can get internet access anywhere the phone has data service, instead of having to rely on WIFI hotspots.

As everyone who hasn't been living under a rock (or perhaps sailing in Mexico) knows, the market is dominated by the Apple iPhone and Android devices. Microsoft is too late, and has never done well in this space despite a decade of trying. The Blackberry is quickly losing it's status as the darling of the business world - especially as more and more companies allow access to corporate mail from iPhones and Androids. Ultimately this saves companies money as well, at least in the technology sector, as people generally are willing to buy their own personal devices if such access is allowed by corporate policy.

As far as the decision between choosing an Android versus an iPhone? Well, that's a personal choice, just like buying a Toyota versus a Ford, or going to Carl's Jr instead of Taco Bell. For me, there was no debating the decision. I think Apple's hardware design is incredible - they have the best industrial design on the planet with slick and slim devices, and well done user interfaces. The problem, is that when you buy an Apple device you are forced into drinking the cool-aid as you are locked into the Apple way of doing things. I'm not a big fan of that approach, no surprise to those that know my opinions on the Open Source model. With Android you have a choice of many different phones and carriers, plus you can install apps from anywhere - not just the hardware, software, and carrier that Apple has decided is best for me.

Ultimately, I believe the Apple lock-in mentality will be their downfall, and already their market share has been eroded by Android devices as Android just surpassed Apple in market share, a pretty big ramp up considering what a head start Apple had on everyone else in the market.

Now, don't go buying an Android just because it's what I happen to like - make your own decision. There's actually a lot to be said for my old dumbphone - it was cheaper and I didn't waste nearly so much time playing around with it. When we'd go out to eat we'd just talk, and then eat. Now I seem to be checking into facebook, checking into foursquare, checking into yelp, checking into ... :-)
