With the upgrade moratorium lifted, and the need to furnish a new apartment, we've bought a few new home theater bits and integrated old home theater hardware from our storage unit. So now we've got a 47" LCD, BlueRay player, and surround sound. Overall it's great, but digital media restrictions threw an interesting twist into the setup.

Temporary setup - wires and stuff everywhere!

We've had a home theater receiver and compact surround sound speakers in storage for almost 3+1/2 years.  Last summer we listed these on Craigslist, along with a bunch of other household stuff we got rid of prior to our sailing cruise to Mexico. Since they weren't taking up much space, we ultimately decided to keep them, even though they've got a decade or so on them.

The speakers aren't much of an issue, since they are analog devices compatible with any amplifier. The receiver, however, while having two digital inputs, does not support HDMI and all the onboard video switching is analog. What to do about this? Well I will argue there's really been a shift in home theater audio and video topology over the past 5 years. Where the home theater receiver used to be the hub of the network, now it's the TV - so basically instead of doing all the video and audio switching in the receiver, it's now done in the TV. This simplifies things in some ways, as you plug all your inputs to the TV, and all you need is one digital audio cable from the TV to the receiver. HDMI cabling makes it easy, since you just need one cable between devices for both video and audio. The devices communicate to establish and negotiate a compatible configuration. Pretty nifty!

Super simple and great, right? Well, it would be - if digital media restrictions weren't an issue. In our case, our Toshiba HDTV will not output a Dolby Digital audio stream! The TV is perfectly cable of doing so, and there are setup options to do so, but it doesn't work. Why? Straight from the manual:
The DIGITAL AUDIO OUT terminal may not output some digital audio sources because of copy restrictions.
Yep, that's right - our brand new HDTV has decided that we aren't worthy of getting the full digital audio stream that our DVD player and cable box are capable of!

I don't care so much about the cable audio, but why have BlueRay and a great home theater setup when we are restricted to ProLogic surround? To get around this was simply a matter of running a separate digital audio output from the DVD player directly into the home theater receiver. And this means an additional step is required to playback a DVD in the best audio, as the receiver has to be switched to the input.

And, as with most of these ridiculous digital media restrictions, it does absolutely nothing to prevent me from 'stealing' the audio bitstream if so desired - as I can record directly off the DVDs digital audio output if I want to.

Digital media rights and restrictions is a worthy topic for a future post, so I'll leave things there for now.
