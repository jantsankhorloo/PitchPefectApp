--- layout: post title: "LightSquared Granted Potential GPS Interference Waiver by FCC" date: 2011-03-15T21:24:00-07:00 comments: true categories: - gps - communication ---
This is very interesting - the company LightSquared was recently granted a waiver by the FCC to install 40,000 terrestrial broadcasting stations in the US that will likely interfere with GPS. These stations will be allowed to broadcast on the radio spectrum adjacent to the GPS spectrum.

In testing so far, GPS signals have been found to be completely jammed at up to around 6 miles from the LightSquared sites.

As much as I understand the issues with wireless data broadband capacity, allowing a waiver which will result in jamming of GPS signals is not the way to go.

Read more here: http://www.gpsworld.com/gnss-system/news/data-shows-disastrous-gps-jamming-fcc-approved-broadcaster-11029
And here: http://www.saveourgps.org

This is right up there with trading away the 70cm HAM bands. Something is seriously wrong with the FCC...
