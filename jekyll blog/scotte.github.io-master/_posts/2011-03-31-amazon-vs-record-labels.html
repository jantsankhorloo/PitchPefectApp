There's a battle brewing between Amazon and the record labels over amazon's new cloud player, which lets you upload your MP3s to the cloud, and play them anywhere you have internet access over a computer or Android phone.

You can upload 5GB of MP3s for free. Higher storage capacities cost 20GB for $20/year, and so on.

Apparently the record labels are having quite a fit over this service, even though it only lets you play music that's you've uploaded yourself. It shows that the record companies have absolutely no clue at all, actually. If it's legal to play from my portable player, and it's legal to play from my computer, then it sure seems to me it's legal to play from my Amazon Cloud Drive, or whatever/wherever.

The fact that the record companies have not figured out how to do business in the digital world is the primary reason that I stopped buying any music, other than what I can legally get DRM free.

On a related note, I just finished converting (again, perfectly legally) Chris' entire music collection over from iTunes format to MP3. Now she can listen to her music anywhere on any device. She's happy to be free of iTunes horrible interface and software, using winamp currently to manage her collection and device (it works nicely, I have to say). Freedom to do what we want with our own, legally acquired content, is a good thing!
